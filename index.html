Frequently Asked Questions

What types of permits does the Engineering Department issue?

The Engineering Department handles sidewalk/driveway permits, right-of-way usage, grading, excavation, and utility connection permits. For building permits, HVAC, or electrical work, contact the Building Division.

Do I need a permit to replace my driveway?

If your work affects public infrastructure like sidewalks or streets, yes. However, flatwork on private property outside of the right-of-way typically does not require a permit.

What projects generally require building permits?

New construction, decks, additions, solar panels, basement finishing, electrical rewiring, plumbing changes, pools, and HVAC replacement all require permits. Sheds over 144 square feet also need permits.

What projects do NOT require permits?

Generally: new floor coverings, painting, wallpapering, replacing light fixtures or faucets, roof shingle replacement, siding replacement, and utility sheds under 144 square feet without electricity.

How do I apply for a permit?

You can apply online or visit the office in person. All payments can be made online by check or credit card with no additional fees for online payments.

How long does the approval process take?

Sidewalk/driveway permits and right-of-way usage can often be acquired within a day. Walk-ups are typically issued same day. Other permits are usually reviewed within one week.

Do I need a permit for a dumpster in the street?

Yes, a Right-of-Way Usage permit is required for dumpsters, PODs, or cranes placed in the street. Type 2 lighted barricades and traffic control are required. No permit needed if placed in your driveway.

How wide can my driveway be?

Per ordinance, residential driveways cannot exceed 29 feet in aggregate width. Commercial and industrial properties have different limits based on use.
